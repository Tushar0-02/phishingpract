What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack method where malicious actors attempt to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data. These attacks often involve impersonating a trustworthy entity, like a bank, government agency, or well-known company, to gain the victim's trust.


### Common Types of Phishing:
1. **Email Phishing**: Fraudulent emails that appear to come from legitimate organizations, urging the recipient to click on a link or provide personal information.
2. **Spear Phishing**: Targeted attacks directed at specific individuals or organizations, often using personalized information to appear more convincing.
3. **Smishing**: Phishing via SMS or text messages.
4. **Vishing**: Voice phishing, where attackers make phone calls pretending to be from trusted organizations.
5. **Clone Phishing**: Duplicating a legitimate message but replacing links or attachments with malicious ones.
6. **Pharming**: Redirecting users to fake websites by compromising DNS servers or infecting devices.


### Signs of Phishing:
- Unexpected messages or emails from unknown sources.
- Urgency or threats to take immediate action.
- Poor grammar or spelling in messages.
- Suspicious links (hover over the link to verify its destination).
- Requests for sensitive information, like login credentials.


### How to Protect Yourself:
1. **Be cautious**: Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.
2. **Verify the sender**: Contact the organization directly to confirm the legitimacy of the request.
3. **Use two-factor authentication (2FA)**: Adds an extra layer of security to your accounts.
4. **Check website URLs**: Ensure the site uses HTTPS and the URL matches the legitimate site.
5. **Educate yourself**: Stay informed about the latest phishing tactics.
6. **Install security tools**: Use reliable antivirus software and keep your system updated.
